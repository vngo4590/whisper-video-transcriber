# Viral Influencer Skill

Personal system for creating viral content, mastering platform algorithms, and growing as
a VTuber and short-form creator. The full 5-pillar framework applies to every content task.

---

## The 5-Pillar Viral Framework

Every piece of content must be evaluated and built through all 5 pillars:

1. **HOOK** — Did it stop the scroll in ≤3 seconds?
2. **RETENTION** — Did it keep them watching past 30%, 50%, 100%?
3. **ENGAGEMENT TRIGGER** — Did it make them like, comment, share, or save?
4. **ALGORITHM FIT** — Is it formatted correctly for the target platform?
5. **NICHE AUTHENTICITY** — Does it feel true to the creator's identity and niche?

When critiquing content, score each pillar 1–10 and explain why. When creating content, build from Hook → out.

---

## Pillar 1: The Hook (0–3 seconds)

The hook is everything. Algorithms measure **average watch percentage** — if people leave early, the video dies. The first 3 seconds must create an **open loop** (curiosity gap) or **immediate payoff**.

### Hook Types by Effectiveness

| Type | Example | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Shock/Surprise** | "I spent $10,000 on this game and got scammed" | Gaming, Lifestyle |
| **Relatable Pain** | "Tell me you're a night owl without telling me" | Lifestyle, Vlogging |
| **Controversy Bait** | "Unpopular opinion: this VTuber model style is dead" | VTuber, Gaming |
| **Visual Spectacle** | Cut immediately to the craziest moment | Gaming clips, Lifestyle |
| **Direct Address** | "If you're a [X], stop scrolling" | All niches |
| **Cliffhanger In-Scene** | Start at the climax, cut back to explain | Vlogging, Gaming |
| **POV/Roleplay** | "POV: you finally got your dream setup" | Gaming, VTuber |

### Hook Rules
- **Cut the intro** — "hey guys, welcome back" is watched by existing fans, not new ones. TikTok's algorithm measures watch percentage from frame one; an intro is dead time that tanks your completion rate before you've had a chance.
- **Subtitles from the first frame** — 70%+ of TikTok views happen with sound off. If your hook is verbal and has no text, most people never receive it.
- **Visual motion in the first 2 seconds** — the brain is wired to track movement. A fast cut, zoom, or camera shift in the opening keeps the eye engaged long enough for your hook to land.
- **Don't explain the hook** — the curiosity gap only works while it's open. The moment you resolve the tension you created, people have what they came for and leave. Let it breathe.

## Pillar 2: Retention Architecture

Keeping viewers watching is the #1 ranking signal on every platform. Build **retention scaffolding** throughout the video.

### The Retention Stack (short-form ≤60s)

```
0–3s     → HOOK (open loop)
3–10s    → Deepen the premise (why should they care?)
10–30s   → The core payoff begins — escalating interest
30–50s   → Peak moment / punchline / reveal
50–60s   → CTA + re-hook (tease next video or loop back to start)
```

### The Retention Stack (long-form 5–20min)

```
0–30s    → Cold open: best moment of the video
30s–2m   → Context + stakes setting
Every 2–3m → New sub-hook or "but wait" moment
Last 60s → Resolution + strong CTA + tease next video
```

### Retention Techniques

- **Pattern interrupts** — Change camera angle, zoom, sound effect, or text overlay every 5–8s
- **Callbacks** — Reference something said earlier ("remember when I said X?")
- **Micro-cliffhangers** — "And then something happened I did NOT expect..."
- **B-roll stacking** — Never stay on one shot for more than 6–8 seconds
- **Looping content** — Ending that loops back to the beginning = TikTok algorithm gold
- **Chapter anchors** (long-form) — Use YouTube chapters; viewers who jump still count as watches

## Pillar 3: Engagement Triggers

Engagement (comments, shares, saves, likes) amplifies reach. Engineer it deliberately.

### Engagement by Type

**Comments (highest algorithm weight):**
- Ask divisive but safe questions: "Which side are you on?"
- Create "fill in the blank": "My starter pack is ___"
- Controversy + community: "Hot take: [opinion]. Fight me in the comments."
- Read comments aloud in your content → encourages more comments for next video

**Shares (second highest):**
- "Send this to someone who needs to see it"
- Extremely relatable content → people tag friends
- Useful/educational content → people save to send later
- Funny moments → people DM to their friends

**Saves (YouTube/Instagram signal):**
- Listicle content: "5 settings that changed my gaming setup"
- Tutorial or reference content
- Aesthetic content people want to revisit

**Likes:**
- Good content gets likes automatically. Don't over-engineer this.

### Engagement CTA Rules
- **One CTA per video** — when you ask for multiple things (like, comment, follow, share), people experience decision paralysis and do none of them. Pick the action that matters most for where you are in your growth stage and ask only for that.
- **Place the CTA at the peak emotional moment, not the end** — by the time most viewers reach your outro, a significant portion have already left. Hit them when they're most engaged and most likely to act.
- **Make it feel like participation, not marketing** — "Drop a 🐣 if you've done this" works because it invites the viewer into the video. "Like and subscribe" signals that you want something from them.

## Pillar 4: Algorithm Mastery by Platform

Read the relevant section based on where the content is being posted.
See `references/algorithms.md` for deep-dive per-platform mechanics.

### Quick Reference: Platform Priorities

| Platform | #1 Signal | #2 Signal | #3 Signal | Ideal Length |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **TikTok** | Completion rate | Shares | Comments | 15–34s (clips), 3–5min (story) |
| **YouTube Shorts** | Swipe-away rate (inverse) | Likes | Subscribers gained | 30–55s |
| **Instagram Reels** | Shares to Stories | Saves | Watch time | 7–15s (viral), 30–60s (content) |
| **YouTube Long-form** | Click-through rate | Watch time % | Return viewers | 8–20min sweet spot |

### Universal Algorithm Rules
1. **Post consistently** — Irregular posting = algorithmic deprioritization
2. **First hour matters** — Engage aggressively with comments in the first 60 minutes
3. **Don't delete and repost** — Editing removes algorithm momentum
4. **Cross-post smartly** — TikTok watermark hurts Instagram/YouTube reach. Use CapCut export without watermark.
5. **Trending sounds = early boost** — Use sounds with <100k videos for best discoverability
6. **Hashtags** — 3–5 targeted hashtags > 20 generic ones

## Pillar 5: Niche Authenticity

Viral content that doesn't feel true to your identity burns out fast. Sustainable virality = niche authority + personality.

### Niche-Specific Content Playbooks

**Gaming:**
- Clip the **reaction**, not just the moment — your face cam during a crazy moment > the moment alone
- "Skill diff" / moments of mastery perform well
- Collabs with other gaming creators compound reach fast
- Rage, shock, and wholesome moments are the 3 emotional pillars of gaming content
- Tier lists and hot takes consistently generate comments

**VTuber:**
- Lean into **lore and character depth** — fans who connect with your persona share more and become advocates, not just viewers
- **Behind-the-scenes content is underutilised gold** — rigging process videos, wing builds from scratch, model reveals, and iteration breakdowns perform strongly because they show work no one else is showing
- **Live2D content has a built-in curiosity hook** — most people have no idea how VTuber models work. "How I built my wings from scratch" is automatically interesting to a wide audience, not just VTubers
- Debut clips, model reveals, and lore drops are high-virality events — plan content around them well in advance
- Clip compilation of best stream moments = low effort, high return — build the habit of flagging moments during streams
- Community events (viewer games, art challenges, design votes for new model elements) drive saves and shares

**Lifestyle:**
- "Day in my life" underperforms without a strong hook — frame as "What I actually do as a [identity]"
- "Routine" content + aesthetic visuals = saves magnet
- Contrast storytelling ("Before vs After", "Expectation vs Reality") is always evergreen
- Authenticity beats polish — unfiltered moments outperform overproduced ones
- Anchor lifestyle content to a specific identity ("as someone who WFH full-time...")

**Vlogging:**
- Story arc is mandatory: Setup → Conflict/Challenge → Resolution
- Travel content: Show the unexpected, not just the pretty
- The more specific the experience, the more universal it feels (paradox of specificity)
- Talking-to-camera charisma compounds over time — study your own watch-through rate

## Content Ideation System

When asked for content ideas, apply this generator:

**Format:** [Trending Format] + [Niche Lens] + [Emotional Hook]

Examples:
- "POV you're a VTuber trying to explain your model to your parents" (POV + VTuber + humor)
- "I tracked every hour of my WFH day for a week and the results are embarrassing" (Data reveal + Lifestyle + relatable shame)
- "Ranking every game I've played this year by how much it hurt me emotionally" (Tier list + Gaming + vulnerability)
- "The part of VTubing nobody talks about" (Taboo reveal + VTuber + curiosity)

**Ideation Triggers to always use:**
1. What's trending in the niche RIGHT NOW? (use this to ride waves, not lag them)
2. What's the most emotionally honest thing I could say about my experience?
3. What would make my past self stop scrolling?
4. What question do viewers always ask me in comments?

## Growth Compounding Strategies

For long-term growth beyond individual viral hits:

- **Series content** — Named series create return viewers (e.g., "Monday Grind Logs")
- **Platform-native collabs** — Duets, stitches, and reply videos leverage others' audiences
- **Comment-to-content pipeline** — Turn top comments into new videos; it's free research
- **Cross-platform funneling** — TikTok/Reels → YouTube long-form → Newsletter/Community
- **Consistency window** — Post at the same time on the same days; train the algorithm AND your audience
- **The 80/20 rule** — 80% content your audience loves, 20% experiments for growth

## Quick Reference Checklist (pre-post)

Before posting any piece of content, check:

- [ ] Hook lands in ≤3 seconds — no intro
- [ ] Subtitles visible from first frame
- [ ] Pattern interrupt every 5–8 seconds
- [ ] One clear CTA placed at peak moment
- [ ] Correct aspect ratio for platform (9:16 for short-form)
- [ ] No TikTok watermark if cross-posting
- [ ] 3–5 targeted hashtags only
- [ ] Trending or royalty-free audio used
- [ ] Thumbnail/cover frame is the most compelling still
- [ ] Caption contains hook text (first line = reason to tap)
